What is CELPE-BRAS?

CELPE-BRAS (Certificado de Proficiência em Língua Portuguesa para Estrangeiros) is Brazil's only officially recognized Portuguese proficiency exam. Administered by INEP under the Ministry of Education, it is offered twice a year at authorized testing centers in Brazil and in over 25 countries worldwide.

Unlike most language proficiency exams, CELPE-BRAS does not use multiple-choice questions. The entire assessment is based on written production and oral interaction, reflecting a communicative approach — the exam measures what you can do with the language, not just what you know about it.

Who needs CELPE-BRAS?

  • University applicants — required for admission to Brazilian undergraduate and graduate programs, including PEC-G and PEC-PG scholarships.
  • Diploma validation — foreign professionals who want to practice their profession in Brazil (doctors, engineers, lawyers) must hold a CELPE-BRAS certificate.
  • Professional registration — required by Brazilian professional councils such as CRM (medicine), OAB (law), and CREA (engineering).
  • Immigration — can be used as proof of Portuguese proficiency in immigration processes.
  • Career advancement — valued by multinational companies operating in Brazil.

Exam structure

Written component (Parte Escrita) — 3 hours

You will complete 4 writing tasks. Each task presents a stimulus — a video clip, audio recording, or written text — and asks you to produce a text in a specific genre: letter to the editor, formal email, opinion article, personal account, among others. You must demonstrate that you can extract information from the stimulus and use it in your text.

Oral component (Parte Oral) — 20 minutes

A face-to-face interaction with an examiner, divided into two parts:

  • 5 minutes — personal conversation about your life, routine, and interests.
  • 15 minutes — discussion prompted by visual and textual stimuli (photos, charts, short texts), where you must express opinions, compare ideas, and argue your point.

Certification levels

Your final certification level is determined by the lowest score between the written and oral components. This means you must prepare both parts equally.

When and where?

CELPE-BRAS is offered twice a year, typically in April and October. Registration opens approximately two months before the exam date through the INEP website. Spots are limited and fill up quickly, especially at international testing centers.

How to prepare

Effective preparation involves three key areas:

  • Master text genres — practice writing letters, formal emails, opinion pieces, and personal accounts.
  • Practice with real stimuli — watch Brazilian news reports, read newspapers, and transform that information into written texts.
  • Train oral interaction — practice expressing opinions, arguing your position, and maintaining a fluent conversation in Portuguese.
