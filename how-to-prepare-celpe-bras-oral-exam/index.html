Overview of the oral exam

The oral component of CELPE-BRAS is a 20-minute face-to-face interaction with an examiner, recorded for later evaluation. It is designed to assess your ability to communicate naturally in Brazilian Portuguese across different topics and contexts.

Part 1: Personal conversation (5 minutes)

The examiner will ask about your daily life, interests, work, studies, and reasons for learning Portuguese. This is not a scripted interview — the examiner will follow up on your answers, so prepared monologues won't work. Treat it like a natural conversation.

Common topics include:

  • Why you are learning Portuguese
  • Your country, culture, and comparisons with Brazil
  • Your professional life or studies
  • Hobbies and daily routine
  • Travel experiences

Part 2: Elementos provocadores (15 minutes)

The examiner presents 3 to 4 visual or textual stimuli — photographs, infographics, advertisements, short texts — and asks you to discuss them. You may need to:

  • Describe what you see
  • Express and justify your opinion
  • Compare situations or cultures
  • Suggest solutions to problems
  • Agree or disagree with a statement

What examiners evaluate

The oral exam is scored on six criteria:

  1. Compreensão — Can you understand the examiner's questions and follow the conversation?
  2. Competência interacional — Can you maintain a natural dialogue, take turns, and respond appropriately?
  3. Fluência — Is your speech smooth, without excessive pauses or hesitation?
  4. Adequação lexical — Do you use varied and precise vocabulary?
  5. Adequação gramatical — Is your grammar accurate for the level?
  6. Pronúncia — Is your pronunciation clear and intelligible?

Practical tips

  • Don't memorize scripts — examiners are trained to detect rehearsed answers. Be natural.
  • Express opinions — the exam rewards candidates who can argue a position, not just describe.
  • Use connectors — "por outro lado," "no entanto," "além disso" show discourse competence.
  • Ask for clarification if needed — saying "Desculpe, poderia repetir?" is better than guessing wrong.
  • Practice with Brazilian media — watching news, podcasts, and TV shows helps internalize natural rhythm and vocabulary.
The oral exam carries equal weight to the written exam. Many candidates focus all their preparation on writing and are caught off guard by the oral component. Don't make this mistake — practice speaking regularly.
