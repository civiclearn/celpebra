What are PEC-G and PEC-PG?

PEC-G (Programa de Estudantes-Convênio de Graduação) and PEC-PG (Programa de Estudantes-Convênio de Pós-Graduação) are Brazilian government scholarship programs that offer study places at Brazilian universities to students from developing countries in Africa, Latin America, Asia, and the Caribbean.

How CELPE-BRAS fits in

CELPE-BRAS is the gateway to both programs. All PEC-G and PEC-PG candidates must obtain a CELPE-BRAS certificate to be eligible for a scholarship. The minimum level required is Intermediário (score 2.00 or above), though some programs or universities may require Intermediário Superior.

The typical timeline

  1. Application — Submit your application through the Brazilian embassy or consulate in your country.
  2. CELPE-BRAS exam — Take the exam at an authorized testing center, usually in April or October.
  3. Results — Receive your certificate, typically 2-3 months after the exam.
  4. University placement — If you meet the language requirement, you are assigned to a Brazilian university.
  5. Portuguese course (optional) — Some candidates are offered a preparatory Portuguese course in Brazil before starting their program.

What if you don't pass?

Some PEC-G candidates who do not achieve the minimum level on CELPE-BRAS may be offered a spot in a Portuguese language course at a Brazilian university. After completing the course, they can retake the exam. However, this is not guaranteed for all countries or all editions of the program.

Preparation tips for PEC-G/PEC-PG candidates

  • Start early — ideally 6 months before the exam date. The Intermediário level is achievable with consistent study.
  • Focus on the written component — most PEC-G candidates struggle more with writing than speaking. Practice the text genres (carta do leitor, e-mail formal, relato).
  • Use Brazilian media — watch TV Globo, TV Brasil, and listen to podcasts. This helps with both listening comprehension and cultural context.
  • Practice with real stimuli — the exam uses authentic Brazilian media as stimuli. Get comfortable extracting information from news reports and articles.
  • Don't underestimate the oral exam — practice having conversations in Portuguese, expressing opinions, and discussing current events.

Countries with the most PEC-G/PEC-PG candidates

The program serves students from over 60 countries, with the largest groups typically coming from Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, Mozambique, Angola, East Timor, Haiti, Paraguay, Bolivia, Colombia, and several countries in West Africa.
