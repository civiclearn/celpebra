Why false friends matter in CELPE-BRAS

Spanish and Portuguese share roughly 85% of their vocabulary, which gives Spanish speakers a massive head start. But this similarity is also a trap: CELPE-BRAS examiners are trained to spot "portunhol" (Spanish-Portuguese mix), and using the wrong word can change the entire meaning of your text.

False friends that appear in CELPE-BRAS tasks

In writing tasks about social issues, education, or daily life, these false friends appear constantly. Imagine writing a formal letter about workplace conditions and using "oficina" instead of "escritório" — the examiner immediately identifies this as Spanish interference.

How to avoid them

  • Build a personal list — every time you discover a false friend, write it down with the correct Portuguese equivalent.
  • Read Brazilian Portuguese daily — immersion naturally corrects these patterns over time.
  • Review before submitting — in the exam, use your final review time to specifically check for Spanish interference.
  • Practice with AI feedback — tools that evaluate your writing can flag Spanish words that slipped through.
